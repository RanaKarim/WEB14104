Traditional Victoria sponge cake recipe

This is the traditional recipe for a Victoria sponge cake, a much loved English favorite.

Main ingredients

  • 3 eggs
  • 150g self raising flour
  • 150g caster sugar
  • 150g butter or margarine
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • strawberry jam
For the butter cream
  • 100g icing sugar
  • 50g butter
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract

Method

  1. Preheat the oven to 170 C / Gas 3. Place the shelf in the center of the oven. Grease and line two 18cm (7 in) sandwich tins with baking parchment.
  2. Weigh the three eggs. Note the weight and measure the same amount of sugar, flour and butter.
  3. Sieve the flour into a bowl and add the sugar, butter or margarine and vanilla. Crack in the eggs and beat well with a wooden spoon or mixer, until the mixture is light colored and fluffy. Divide the cake mixture between the tins and smooth the tops.
  4. Bake for 30 to 40 minutes or until golden brown. Cool for 5 minutes in the tins, then turn out onto a wire rack to cool completely.
  5. To make the butter cream, sieve the sugar into a bowl, add the butter and vanilla and beat well.
  6. To sandwich the cakes together: Add a layer of jam to the top of one of the sponges, followed by a layer of cream on top of the jam, finish by placing the last of the sponges on top. Dust with a layer of icing sugar if desired.

Nutritional info

Extra tips

Use margarine, butter is quite heavy. Eggs can vary a lot in size, for perfect results weigh the 3 eggs. This will more than likely weigh over or under 150g (6 oz). For example, if the eggs weigh 175g you would use 175g each of the flour, sugar and margarine.
